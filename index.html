Before entering to physics, i know i have to learn some programming to became a competitive student which i thought would become part of the burden aside from learning physics and math. To my surprise, i enjoyed programming and mathematics than the actual physics itself which i did not expected at all! Now that i'm a junior student in physics, i really pushed to apply the programming skills that i am learning right now to my researches in physics. My researches right now involve artificial intelligence on the field of medical imaging and in the field of gravitation. I'm also doing some front-end web/software development in React JS on which i spent majority of my free time.
